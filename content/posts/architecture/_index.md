Welcome to the Software Architecture & Design series, where we explore the art and science of building robust, scalable software systems.

## What You'll Find Here

This collection examines the fundamental principles, patterns, and practices that shape how we architect software systems. We go beyond surface-level tutorials to explore the deeper reasoning behind architectural decisions and their long-term implications.

**Core Areas:**
- **System Design Fundamentals**: Principles for designing systems that can evolve and scale
- **Architectural Patterns**: When to use microservices, monoliths, event-driven architectures, and hybrid approaches
- **Legacy System Evolution**: Strategies for modernizing existing systems without breaking them
- **Modularity & Composition**: Building systems from composable, reusable components
- **Distributed Systems**: Handling complexity, consistency, and resilience across network boundaries
- **Performance & Scalability**: Architectural decisions that impact system performance at scale

## The ADHD Engineer's Approach

Many insights here emerge from an ADHD perspective on system design—preferring clear abstractions over complex implementations, seeking patterns that reduce cognitive load, and building systems that work with rather than against how minds process complexity.

This often leads to architectures that are more maintainable, more testable, and more adaptable to changing requirements.

## From Code to Requirements

Architecture isn't just about technical components—it's about understanding the problem space deeply enough to create solutions that align with both current needs and future evolution. We explore how good architecture emerges from the interplay between:

- **Business Context**: Understanding the domain and organizational constraints
- **Technical Constraints**: Working within the bounds of performance, scalability, and maintainability
- **Human Factors**: Designing systems that development teams can understand and evolve
- **Evolution Patterns**: Anticipating how systems will need to change over time

## Real-World Focus

Every architectural discussion here is grounded in practical experience—the messy realities of enterprise systems, the challenges of legacy code, the trade-offs of different approaches, and the lessons learned from both successes and failures.

Whether you're designing your first distributed system or refactoring a decade-old monolith, this series provides frameworks for thinking through architectural challenges and making informed decisions about system design.
